The Elect is a set of small AI voice-models, each fine-tuned on the public-domain writing of a historical figure so it imitates how that person wrote. Six of them were put in a room together for this debate.

About this document

These are outputs from small AI voice-models, each fine-tuned on the public-domain writings of a historical figure (John Brown, Karl Marx, Thomas Müntzer, Franz Kafka, and others) to imitate how that person wrote. The views below are generated text. They are not the positions of anyone living, and they are not mine. Nothing was steered: I did not bake my own politics into these models or push them toward any conclusion. What each voice says is raw, generated from that figure's own corpus and period worldview. I have my own views; they are deliberately not in here. Read this as a creative experiment in historical voice, not as advocacy for anything it says.

But we must judge these things rightly, with our eyes on the heart and not just the outward show: does this building serve the poor or serve itself? And what does Scripture say about such matters?

Isaiah prophesied against those who built high walls of stone to defend their plundered wealth while the widows weep without. Ezekiel called the rulers of Israel 'wolves devoured by dogs' because they fed on God's people and then pretended to protect them from outsiders. Revelation promises a new heavens and earth where the lion lies down with the lamb, not where lions prey more effectively than before. These visions are not wishes — they are warnings against making false gods out of our arrangements for survival. The arrangement we call 'Israel' serves certain interests very well indeed. But if those interests conflict with the interest of justice as revealed in Scripture, then what appears to be a triumph may turn out to be a judgment instead.

I do not wish ill upon any group that calls itself Jewish or Israelite. I wish them wisdom, as God promised to Abraham and his descendants. What I cannot accept is the claim that this arrangement — however many centuries have been invested in it, however many tears shed for its sake — has become sacred simply by virtue of its existence. Sacredness comes from elsewhere: from obedience to what God has spoken about justice between man and man, regardless of race or creed or supposed chosenness. If Israel's leaders choose the path of power over the path of righteousness, then history will record that choice against them, not in their favor.

This is hard teaching, I know it. But it is also the plain word of God as given to us in the prophets and apostles. To deny this word for the sake of comfort or convenience is to betray both the living God and the dead bodies of those who perished under false promises. Let whoever can stand with me on this ground: against all tyrants, for the truth that sets men free, even if that freedom means a new way of suffering rather than an end to it. For no arrangement made by fallible hands is eternal; only the word of God remains faithful through every age."
